Sanitization Redefined.

The IEEE Student Branch Chapter of Rajagiri school of Engineering and Technology took up the #Robosanitizer Challenge in the month of March. Jerin Peter of S2 ECE came up with a project as part of this challenge, gathering fame and recognition in various platforms regarding his advanced prototype oriented towards sanitization of the general public without compromising to contact. The challenge expired long ago, but the IEEE SB of RSET decided that such a fruitful prototype shouldn't go to waste.

Bringing Vision to Reality

Supportive and connected as always, our college's IEEE SB took up this project and saw through its make over into a complete and self sustaining product. By consulting with several startups and resource persons from the ties we maintained over the years, we developed Jerin's product into something truly worthy of any public space imaginable

Contributions

What brings variety to our operation is that our operation is that while our product gained from a single student's project, the roots of its success lie in the contributions of each of the three IEEE societies of our college. We have accepted this venture as one of our own down to an individual level. Here are the contributions of our IEEE Student Branch Chapters towards this venture .

The Power and Energy Society of RSET is the one that accepted the #Robosanitizer challenge in our stead when the movement was popular. Besides this, the CTO Jerin Peter, had come up with his project only due to the platform provided by the IEEE PES SBC of RSET.

The Industrial Applications society was the first society to suggest bringing the robosanitizer out into the open market. Through their tremendous network of startups, they were the key player in the marketing aspect of Touch Me Not.

The Computer Society is the main pillar in the advertising of our product all over the world. They have actively participated in gathering sponsorships and sellers for Touch Me Not from all over the world despite this global pandemic.

Advantages

Having a "Touch Me Not" at home or at work can have a high impact on your general health and cleanliness. Here's a few areas where our product proves its worth.

Keep Clean

Touch Me Not is essentially a way to apply sanitizer without ever touching a surface. This significantly reduces the risk of exposure to the Novel Corona virus, thereby preventing disease spread at minimum cost.
